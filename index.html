Mission & Vision

Vision: verify safety independently

A lot of sensitive information is (required to be) given out to organizations. These organizations have the obligation to handle this data responsibly. In many cases the owner of the data is asked to trust those organization, blindly.

Our mission is to remove blind trust from of the equation: everybody should be able to determine themselves if their data is handled responsibly.

Mission: give the means to verify

The Internet Cleanup Foundation develops tools that helps the public in making sure that organizations which are required to protect information of others is doing their job correctly.

Hundreds of issues solved and counting

Fail map is the project that spawned this foundation. It is a geographical map that shows how well digital safety is implemented in organizations tasked with handling personal data.

By using the judgemental term "failure", organizations are quicker to respond to their failure. Combined with the extremely clear visual aesthetic of the fail map, anyone can determine who is doing their job.

The map knows four colors:

  • Red: insecure, obvious problems
  • Orange: bound to be insecure soon
  • Green: cannot find anything insecure
  • Gray: nothing to check

The information on the map is gathered automatically, every day. This process can only look at a very basic level of safety, representative of the rest of the tested organization. The basic and often mandated level of safety originates from best practices, standards bodies, governments, companies and groups with technical know-how.
